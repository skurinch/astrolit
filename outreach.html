Storytelling for Scientists

As a writer, I am enthusiastic about sharing my own stories as well. I crafted narratives with the Storytelling for Scientists group at Caltech about my passion for astronomy in order to start conversations about the role of science in society. Through various showcases, I shared the summer I learned to use a research-grade telescope, and the relation between astronomy and medieval art. Recently, I created a play about the role of women in climate science which was performed for the public at the Museo delle Scienze in Italy. Through connecting science with topics that are more personable to the general public—from societal problems to art—I want to to communicate science in a way that reaches people and allows them to experience astronomy as relevant and inspiring.

Astronomy Storytime for Kids


“Out of this World!” at the Library

I want to bring stories to others. During my Fulbright, I designed an astronomy-themed storytime for kids at the German-American Insitute library in Heidelberg to teach them literacy skills and encourage a lifelong love for science and reading. Together, we read picture books about planets, stars and constellations, “girls who do science,” and galaxies. Working with these kids is about helping them access stories about the Universe no matter the context: from outer space, to fairytales, to family. The DAI-Heidelberg gave me the opportunity to engage with bright and driven children from different parts of the world.

Reach the World: Astronomy Travel Diaries

Through Reach the World, I wrote blog articles about my astronomy research and travels in Germany for an elementary school classroom in New York City. I also collaborated with the teachers to set up video conferences over year, where the students could ask questions about the culture in Europe, and also what it's like to be an astrophysicist. The kids were really excited about astronomy, and loved black holes!
